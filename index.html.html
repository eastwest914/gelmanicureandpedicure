Frequently Asked Questions

A Russian Gel Manicure is a specialized nail technique that uses an electric file (e-file) to meticulously prep and clean the cuticle area, resulting in an ultra-precise, flawless finish. Unlike a traditional gel manicure, the Russian method removes excess cuticle and dead skin around the nail bed before applying gel, which allows the polish to adhere much closer to the skin. The result is a cleaner, more polished look that lasts significantly longer — often up to 3–4 weeks — with minimal lifting. At Gel Manicure And Pedicure in Queens, our technicians are fully trained in this advanced technique to give you the best possible results.
One of the biggest advantages of a Russian Gel Manicure is its exceptional longevity. When done correctly by a skilled technician, a Russian Gel Manicure can last anywhere from 3 to 5 weeks without chipping, peeling, or significant lifting. The meticulous prep work involved in the Russian technique allows the gel to bond directly to the nail surface, significantly extending wear time compared to a standard gel manicure. To maximize longevity, we recommend keeping your hands moisturized and wearing gloves when doing dishes or heavy cleaning. Shellac is actually a specific brand of gel-polish hybrid product made by CND (Creative Nail Design). It combines the ease of regular nail polish application with the durability of gel, curing under UV/LED light for a chip-resistant, high-gloss finish. Shellac is thinner and more flexible than traditional hard gel, making it ideal for clients who want a natural-feeling, lightweight manicure with long-lasting color. Standard gel nails tend to be thicker and more durable.
